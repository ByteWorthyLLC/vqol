# VEINES-QOL/Sym Citation Pack

Reference pack for the LSHTM inquiry, README, scoring documentation, and clinician PDF export.

## Primary Instrument Papers

### 1. Lamping et al. 2003 — Instrument development and original validation
**Full citation:**
Lamping DL, Schroter S, Kurz X, Kahn SR, Abenhaim L. Evaluation of outcomes in chronic venous disorders of the leg: development of a scientifically rigorous, patient-reported measure of symptoms and quality of life. *J Vasc Surg.* 2003;37(2):410–419.
- DOI: [10.1067/mva.2003.152](https://doi.org/10.1067/mva.2003.152)
- LSHTM Research Online: https://researchonline.lshtm.ac.uk/id/eprint/16944/
- PMID: 12563212 (confirm via PubMed — DOI is authoritative)
- **Contains:** Full item text (26 items), response options, item coding, four validated language versions (English, French, Italian, French Canadian), psychometric validation results, scoring method description. This is the canonical source for item text.

### 2. Kahn et al. 2006 — DVT-specific validation (VETO Study)
**Full citation:**
Kahn SR, Lamping DL, Ducruet T, Arsenault L, Miron MJ, Roussin A, Desmarais S, Joyal F, Kassis J, Solymoss S, Desjardins L, Johri M, Shrier I; VETO Study investigators. VEINES-QOL/Sym questionnaire was a reliable and valid disease-specific quality of life measure for deep venous thrombosis. *J Clin Epidemiol.* 2006;59(10):1049–1056.
- DOI: [10.1016/j.jclinepi.2005.10.016](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jclinepi.2005.10.016)
- LSHTM Research Online: https://researchonline.lshtm.ac.uk/11090/
- Erratum: Kahn SR et al. *J Clin Epidemiol.* 2006;59(12):1334. DOI: 10.1016/j.jclinepi.2006.10.013
- **Contains:** DVT-specific psychometric validation; French and English versions. Does not republish item text but confirms scoring validity in a DVT cohort. Scoring constants (means, SDs) for the DVT validation sample appear here.

## Scoring Manual (Gray Literature)

### 3. Lamping & Schroter 2007 — Official Scoring Manual
**Full citation:**
Lamping DL, Schroter S. *VEINES-QOL/VEINES-SYM Scoring Manual.* London: London School of Hygiene & Tropical Medicine; 2007.
- Not publicly available online. Must be requested directly from LSHTM / Sara Schroter.
- **Contains:** Item-level normative means and standard deviations from the original VEINES validation sample; scoring algorithm for converting raw item scores to standardized scale scores; instructions for calculating VEINES-QOL and VEINES-Sym subscale scores.
- This is the key document for implementing the scoring algorithm. Without it, scores cannot be expressed in the standard SD-unit format used in published literature.
- Note: The 2015 BMC Cardiovasc Disord paper (Bland et al., PMC4531536) attempted to obtain this data from LSHTM and could not after Prof. Lamping's death — confirming the manual is held by surviving authors / LSHTM, not publicly archived.

## Subsequent Validation Papers (language versions and populations)

### 4. German/French validation in elderly DVT patients
Tritschler T, Méan M, Limacher A, Rodondi N, Aujesky D. Reliability and validity of the VEINES-QOL/Sym questionnaire in elderly patients with deep vein thrombosis. *Qual Life Res.* 2014;23(8):2463–2472.
- PMID: 24791928
- DOI: 10.1007/s11036-014-0532-2
- **Contains:** German-language version psychometric validation. Confirms German as a validated translation.

### 5. Norwegian validation
Utne I, Tavoly M, Wester AL, Jørgensen LN, Sandset PM, Dahm AE, Heir T. Assessing burden of illness following acute deep vein thrombosis: data quality, reliability and validity of the Norwegian version of VEINES-QOL/Sym. *Scand J Caring Sci.* 2009;23(3):459–466.
- DOI: 10.1111/j.1471-6712.2008.00618.x
- **Contains:** Norwegian-language validation.

### 6. Venous leg ulcer validation (intrinsic scoring method)
Bland JM, Dumville JC, Ashby RL, Gabe R, Stubbs N, Adderley U, Kang'ombe AR, Cullum NA. Validation of the VEINES-QOL quality of life instrument in venous leg ulcers: repeatability and validity study embedded in a randomised clinical trial. *BMC Cardiovasc Disord.* 2015;15:85.
- DOI: [10.1186/s12872-015-0080-7](https://doi.org/10.1186/s12872-015-0080-7)
- PMC: [PMC4531536](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4531536/)
- PMID: 26260973
- License: CC BY 4.0 (the *paper* is open access; the instrument item text within it retains LSHTM copyright)
- **Contains:** First venous leg ulcer-specific validation; introduces intrinsic scoring method; proposes a new scoring approach that enables between-study comparison without requiring the Lamping 2007 normative data. This paper is important for the fallback scoring approach.
- Also confirms: Prof. Lamping was deceased at time of publication; Sara Schroter acknowledged for assistance with validation data access.

### 7. Danish electronic adaptation (REDCap)
Hald EM, Brækkan SK, Gabrielsen RG, Grimnes G, Christiansen SC, Ghanima W, Jensvoll H. Electronic adaptation and danish cross-cultural translation of PEmb-QoL and VEINES-QoL/Sym for patients with venous thromboembolism. *J Patient Rep Outcomes.* 2024;8:19.
- DOI: 10.1186/s41687-024-00698-9
- PMC: [PMC10897079](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10897079/)
- **Contains:** Electronic (REDCap) adaptation precedent; Danish translation. Important as evidence that instrument has been deployed in electronic/digital form by academic groups — establishes that LSHTM has implicitly tolerated digital redistribution in academic contexts.

## Existing Commercial/Institutional Bundling — Licensing Precedents

These demonstrate that instrument rights holders have previously authorized bundling in electronic platforms:

1. **ePROVIDE / Mapi Research Trust** — Lists VEINES-QOL/Sym in their COA catalog (https://eprovide.mapi-trust.org/instruments/venous-insufficiency-epidemiological-and-economic-study-veines-quality-of-life-symptoms). Mapi acts as licensing agent for COA developers; the instrument being listed here implies the rights holder (LSHTM) has an active licensing arrangement through Mapi. This is the most important precedent: LSHTM has already engaged with a commercial PRO licensing infrastructure.

2. **REDCap implementations** — Multiple academic groups (Danish, German, Norwegian) have distributed electronic versions of VEINES-QOL/Sym via REDCap to collaborating institutions without published evidence of per-use fees, suggesting non-commercial academic use has been implicitly or explicitly permitted.

3. **VenUS IV trial (UK)** — Used VEINES-QOL/Sym in a large NIHR-funded RCT (ISRCTN49373072) without published licensing disclosure, consistent with non-commercial research use being routinely permitted.
